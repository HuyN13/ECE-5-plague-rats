What does PID stand for? What is the purpose of the P, the I, and the D? What steps did you take to choose the best values for your robot? Explain this like you would for someone taking this course next year.

  PID stand for Preportional, Intergral, Derivative, and is used with a potentiometer circuit in a control loop. This loop is constantly receiving feedback from the circuit. You can change the P by changing the proportional gain constant. The purpose of P is to output a value that is equivalent to the error value. Integral is important because it measures the error over time and gives the accumulated balance. Derivative improves on the stability and control of the robot, which limits high frequencies. The steps we took to choose the best values for our robot is initially putting everything at 0, then continue to test the robot while shifting the speed up, along with the PID.
